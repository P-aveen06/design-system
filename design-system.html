Color

Neutrals carry the page. A single restrained accent draws attention to actions and selection. Four semantic colors signal status only — never decoration. Click any swatch to copy its hex, or click the swatch itself to pick a custom color for that token.

Primary

The brand accent and its derived variants. Use for primary actions, links, focus rings, and selection. Pick anything — the rest of the page derives around it.

Neutral

The grays that carry 95% of any interface — backgrounds, text, borders. Each token is named by its role (bg, surface, border, text-muted), not by shade. That's what lets layouts read the same regardless of brand, and what makes dark mode "just work."

Semantic

Four status colors with fixed meanings: success (green), warning (amber), danger (red), info (blue). They stay recognizable regardless of your brand accent — so a "delete" button always reads as destructive, never as decoration.
